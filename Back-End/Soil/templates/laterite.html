Soil Description

Origin and Composition: Characterized by high iron and aluminium content, formed in hot and wet tropical areas. It is red to brown in color, with a tendency to harden when exposed to air.

Properties: Highly acidic and poor in organic matter, phosphorus, and nitrogen but can be very fertile with proper management.

Crops We can Grow in this Soil

Tea, Coffee, and Cashews: These crops thrive well in acidic conditions prevalent in laterite soils.

Rubber and Coconut: Also suitable for cultivation in these soils, especially in regions with adequate rainfall.

Irrigation Methods

Contour Bunding: Helps in water conservation and prevents soil erosion in sloped areas.

Drip or Sprinkle Irrigation: Minimizes water loss and helps in maintaining optimal soil moisture.

Precaution

Soil Amendment: Application of lime can reduce acidity, and organic matter should be regularly added to improve fertility.

Erosion Control: Steps should be taken to prevent soil erosion, especially in areas prone to heavy rains.
