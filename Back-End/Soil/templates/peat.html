Soil Description

Origin and Composition: Composed primarily of decomposed organic matter, it is dark, rich, and has a spongy texture. Found in waterlogged and marshy areas.

Properties: High in organic content and moisture, with acidic pH levels.

Crops We can Grow in this Soil

Acid-Loving Plants: Suitable for growing cranberries, blueberries, and certain ornamental plants that thrive in acidic conditions.

Rice: Can be cultivated in controlled waterlogged conditions.

Irrigation Methods

Controlled Waterlogging: In case of rice cultivation, maintaining a shallow layer of water is beneficial.

Subsurface Irrigation: Can be effective for other crops, minimizing water loss and maintaining humidity.

Precaution

Water Management: Critical to prevent over-saturation or drying out. Drainage systems may be needed to control water levels.

Acidic Nature Management: Liming can be used to raise the pH for growing a broader range of crops.
